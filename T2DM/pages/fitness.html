Aerobic Exercise

This is activity that raises your heart rate and makes you breathe harder. It's great for your heart, lungs, and blood sugar control.

Goal: 150 minutes per week

Examples:

  • Brisk Walking (you can still talk, but not sing)
  • Cycling or Stationary Bike
  • Swimming or Water Aerobics
  • Dancing or Zumba
  • Jogging

Strength Training

This involves making your muscles work against resistance. Building muscle helps your body use glucose more effectively.

Goal: 2-3 sessions per week

Examples:

  • Lifting weights (dumbbells, barbells)
  • Using resistance bands
  • Using weight machines at a gym
  • Bodyweight exercises (squats, push-ups, lunges)

Flexibility & Balance

These activities help keep your muscles limber and can reduce your risk of falling by improving your stability.

Goal: Incorporate regularly

Examples:

  • Stretching (holding each stretch for 10-30 seconds)
  • Yoga
  • Tai Chi

Tips for Success

Getting Started Safely

  • Talk to your doctor: Discuss your fitness plans to make sure they're right for you.
  • Start slow: If you're new to exercise, begin with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase your time.
  • Check your blood sugar: Test before and after exercise to see how your body responds.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.

Staying Motivated

  • Find something you enjoy: You're more likely to stick with an activity you find fun.
  • Schedule it: Treat your workouts like appointments you can't miss.
  • Exercise with a friend: A partner can help keep you accountable and make it more enjoyable.
  • Don't give up: If you miss a day, just get back on track the next day. Progress over perfection!
