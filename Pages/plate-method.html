The Diabetes Plate Method

The simplest way to create healthy meals that can help manage blood sugar.

No Counting, No Weighing, No Measuring!

The Plate Method is a visual way to make sure you get enough non-starchy vegetables and lean protein while managing the amount of carbohydrate foods you eat. All you need is a 9-inch plate!

1

Fill Half Your Plate with Non-Starchy Vegetables

These veggies are low in carbs and high in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They help you feel full and keep your blood sugar stable.

Examples:

  • Asparagus
  • Broccoli & Cauliflower
  • Cabbage & Kale
  • Carrots
  • Cucumber & Salad Greens
  • Mushrooms
  • Peppers
  • Tomatoes
  • Zucchini
2

Fill One Quarter with Lean Protein Foods

Protein helps keep you satisfied and is important for muscle health. Choose plant-based or lean animal sources.

Examples:

  • Chicken or turkey (skinless)
  • Fish like salmon, tuna, cod
  • Shellfish like shrimp, scallops
  • Lean beef or pork cuts
  • Eggs
  • Tofu & Tempeh
  • Beans, lentils, and hummus
  • Nuts and nut butters
3

Fill One Quarter with Carbohydrate Foods

Carbs provide energy. Choosing high-fiber, whole-grain options helps manage blood sugar better.

Examples:

  • Whole grains like brown rice, quinoa, oats
  • Whole wheat bread, pasta, tortillas
  • Starchy vegetables like corn, peas, sweet potatoes
  • Fruit (all types)
  • Milk and yogurt

This is a simplified guide. For more detailed information, recipes, and tips, please visit the official page from the American Diabetes Association.
